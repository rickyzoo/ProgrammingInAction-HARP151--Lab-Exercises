Economic Analysis of Food Price Inflation's Impact on Crime

This data analysis project was my final project for ECON416 - Economic Analysis with Python at SUNY Binghamton.

In this project, I examine the relationship between food price inflation and the movement of crime in food secure and food insecure states.
I focus on food price inflation of different food baskets (Meat/Protein, Fruits & Vegetables, Dairy, etc) and crime data from 2 food secure and 2 food insecure states in order to examine potential relationships and correlations between the two issues.

About the Data

As mentioned earlier, I focus on crime rates in 2 food secure (Virginia & North Dakota) and 2 food insecure (Mississippi & Alabama) states. The crime data was taken directly from Crime Data Explorer, a government-operated website associated with the FBI. Crime data was broken down into violent and property crime rates per 100,000 people.
Provided below are direct links to the CSV files hosted on my Github repository containing data for each type of crime for each state:

  • Property Crime in Alabama
  • Violent Crime in Alabama
  • Property Crime in Mississippi
  • Violent Crime in Mississippi
  • Property Crime in North Dakota
  • Violent Crime in North Dakota
  • Property Crime in Virginia
  • Violent Crime in Virginia

I was able to differentiate food secure vs. food insecure states by examining food insecurity data from SocialPolicyLab.org, a data visualization research lab focused on economics and policy making content.

Finally, I used CPI data from the FRED, the ever-so-beloved website of Economists, to calculate food price inflation.
